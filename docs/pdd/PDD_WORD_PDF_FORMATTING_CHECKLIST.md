## 4. Word Heading Style Guidance

- Use title/subtitle styling for `Project Design Document` and `Runiac`, not numbered heading styles.
- Use Word `Heading 1` for sections 1 to 7.
- Use Word `Heading 2` for subsections such as 2.1, 3.1, 6.1, and 7.1.
- Use Word `Heading 3` for wireframe figure group headings if they should appear in the Table of Contents.
- Keep heading numbering document-wide and consistent with the Markdown draft.
- After applying styles, generate or refresh the Table of Contents from Word heading styles.

## 5. Caption and List of Figures Guidance

- Use Word's `Insert Caption` feature for every main figure from Figure 1 to Figure 28.
- Keep figure numbering document-wide, not chapter-based.
- For Figures 5 to 15, use subfigure labels such as Figure 5a and Figure 5b near the images only if useful, but use one main Word caption for the group.
- Convert Markdown `**Caption:**` text into normal Word captions or caption-adjacent explanatory text.
- Do not let image alt text become separate visible captions.
- Generate the List of Figures from Word caption fields after all captions are inserted.
- Refresh the Table of Contents and List of Figures immediately before PDF export.

## 6. Page Break Recommendations

Consider page breaks at these points:

- After the title page.
- After the Table of Contents and List of Figures.
- Before each major section from Section 1 to Section 7.
- Before Section 6, because wireframes are image-heavy.
- Before Section 6.4, because administrator wireframes begin a different governance view.
- Before Section 6.5, because Medical Trainer/Expert wireframes begin a different actor view.
- Before the Appendix.

Layout notes:

- Place Figures 3 and 4 full-width or on landscape pages if portrait layout makes labels hard to read.
- Keep each image close to its caption and related explanatory paragraph.
- Avoid splitting grouped figure captions away from their image groups.
- Check that tables do not split awkwardly across pages.

## 7. PDF Export Checklist

Before exporting to PDF:

- Update all Word fields.
- Refresh the Table of Contents.
- Refresh the List of Figures.
- Check page numbering.
- Check figure numbering from Figure 1 to Figure 28.
- Confirm Figures 3 and 4 are readable.
- Confirm no image is cropped, stretched, or heavily compressed.
- Confirm captions remain with the correct figure.
- Run Word spelling and grammar checks.
- Export to PDF using high-quality image settings where available.
- Open the exported PDF and inspect title page, contents pages, diagrams, wireframes, captions, and final appendix.
